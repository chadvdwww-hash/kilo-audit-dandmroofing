Executive Summary

D&M Roofing operates from a Nebraska HQ (Waterloo, NE) and uses texas.dandmroofing.com as a subdomain for its Texas division. The site has solid bones: 44+ pages, decent content volume averaging 2,800 words per page, clean URL structure, and a reasonable schema range including LocalBusiness, VideoObject, and BreadcrumbList.

The critical problem: the LocalBusiness schema on every single page lists the Nebraska address (23920 Cedar Hollow Mall, Waterloo, NE) instead of a Texas address. Google thinks this is a Nebraska business. Every page tells Google the wrong state. Until this is fixed, no amount of content or technical optimization will move the needle on Texas rankings.

Open Graph and Twitter Card tags are completely absent across all 11 audited pages. H1 tags are missing on 9 of 11 pages, with H2 used as the primary heading instead. Meta descriptions are missing on 7 of 11 pages. Title tags on 7 of 11 pages reference "Nebraska & Texas" on what should be a Texas-only subdomain. The phone number displayed is a Nebraska area code (402). Service area pages are templated with no unique local content.

The blog/post sitemap contains a single default WordPress post from 2018. There is zero educational or informational content targeting the Texas market. No storm damage page exists despite Texas being a top-5 state for hail damage.

Strengths worth preserving: good schema variety (just needs correct geographic data), strong content volume per page, clean URL structure, and a large service area page footprint covering 20+ Houston metro cities.

Patterns of the Top 10%

What the highest-ranking Texas roofing companies do consistently. Each pattern includes a specific action for D&M.

1
Local Phone Number and NAP Consistency

Winners have a local area code phone number on every page, in schema, in GBP, consistent everywhere. D&M shows a Nebraska 402 area code on the Texas site. Google uses phone number locality as a ranking signal for local search.

Action: Get a Texas phone number (713/832/512 area code) and update site-wide.

2
Dedicated Storm Damage + Insurance Pages

Texas is a hail and storm market. Every top competitor has dedicated storm damage and insurance claim assistance pages. D&M has neither. These pages have the highest conversion rates in roofing because the searcher has an active problem.

Action: Create /storm-damage/ and /insurance-claims/ pages immediately.

3
Google Reviews as Trust Accelerator

Longhorn has 200+ reviews. Ace has 300+. D&M shows 46 reviews at 4.9 stars, but schema references Nebraska. Texas customers see no local reviews. Google's local ranking factors weight review count and recency heavily.

Action: Build a Texas-specific GBP listing and review collection process.

4
Static/Fast Sites Beat WordPress Bloat

Top Austin competitors load in under 2 seconds. D&M's Elementor build loads 3MB+ per page. A static site generator (Astro, Next.js) would cut page weight by 80%. Page speed is a confirmed ranking factor, especially on mobile.

Action: Consider rebuilding on a static framework, or aggressively strip Elementor bloat.

5
Exact-Match or Keyword-Rich Domain Wins

1st Capital owns roofingcompanyaustin.com. texas.dandmroofing.com is a subdomain of a Nebraska company. Subdomains inherit less authority than root domains and are treated as separate entities by Google.

Action: Consider a dedicated Texas domain (e.g., dandmroofingtexas.com) for stronger signal.

Recommended Architecture Changes

1
Add /storm-damage/ Landing Page

Highest-priority missing page. Target keywords: "storm damage roof repair texas", "hail damage roof repair houston". Include insurance claim assistance content, emergency contact CTA, before/after photos. Link from homepage and all service area pages.

2
Add /roof-repair/ and /roof-replacement/ Pages

Split these out of /residential-services/. Each targets different search intent and keyword clusters. "Roof repair houston tx" and "roof replacement houston tx" are both high-volume, high-intent keywords that need dedicated landing pages.

3
Launch /blog/ Content Hub

Start with 4 cornerstone posts: "How Much Does Roof Replacement Cost in Texas", "Signs You Need a New Roof", "Best Roofing Materials for Texas Heat", "How to File a Roof Insurance Claim in Texas". Publish 2-4 posts per month to build topical authority.

4
Expand Service Area Coverage

Add area pages for Austin, Dallas, and San Antonio if D&M services those metros. Each page needs 500+ words of unique local content, local schema, and location-specific testimonials. Do not duplicate the templated approach of current pages.

Strategic Recommendation

Priority 1: Fix schema geographic data. Every page tells Google this is a Nebraska business. The LocalBusiness JSON-LD on all pages lists 23920 Cedar Hollow Mall, Waterloo, NE. Until the schema, phone number, and address are updated to Texas, no amount of content or technical optimization will help Texas rankings. This is the single highest-impact fix.

Priority 2: Add H1 tags and meta descriptions site-wide. 9 of 11 pages have no H1, and 7 of 11 have no meta description. These are table-stakes SEO elements that every competitor has in place.

Priority 3: Add Open Graph and Twitter Card tags. Zero social sharing metadata means every link shared on social platforms shows a blank preview. This kills referral traffic and brand perception.
